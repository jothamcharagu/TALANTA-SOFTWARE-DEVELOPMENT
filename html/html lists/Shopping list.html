Cooking Glossary
Firm to the bite:
Pasta that is cooked until soft on the outside but still slightly chewy in the middle.
Blend / Bind:
Mixing two ingredients together that usually separate—like hot pork fat and starchy water—to create a smooth, creamy sauce.
Melting fat:
Cooking meat slowly over low heat to melt the solid fat into liquid while leaving the meat crispy.
Cured pork jowl:
A traditional Italian bacon made from the pig's cheek, cured with salt and spices.
